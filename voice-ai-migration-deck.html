Engineering Assessment

Replacing Retell AI

Voice quality, transcription accuracy, and provider flexibility — evaluating VAPI, LiveKit, Pipecat, Bolna, and Twilio ConversationRelay as alternatives for the WIN AI Frontdesk.

Zoca Engineering | April 2026 | Confidential
The Problem

Why We're Evaluating Alternatives

Three core issues with Retell that directly impact client experience.

🗣

Voice Quality

Voices sound noticeably AI-generated. Callers immediately recognize they're talking to a bot, reducing trust and engagement. Limited TTS provider options on Retell.

Client-Facing Impact
📝

Transcription Quality

STT accuracy is poor — names, services, and appointment details are frequently misheard. Leads to wrong bookings, missed context, and frustrated callers.

Booking Accuracy Impact
🔒

Provider Lock-in

Retell locks us into their chosen STT and TTS providers. Very few options to choose from. Cannot test better alternatives (Deepgram Nova-3, ElevenLabs, Cartesia).

Flexibility Constraint
⚠ Bottom line: All three problems are provider flexibility problems. Retell doesn't let us choose the best STT or TTS. The fix = a platform that lets us swap providers independently.

Recommendation

Proposed Path Forward

Phase 1 — Quick Win (Weeks 1–4)

V
Migrate to VAPI
  • →Same architecture — minimal risk, fastest path
  • →Immediately test Deepgram Nova-3 + ElevenLabs / Cartesia
  • →Validates whether better STT/TTS actually solves the problem
  • →1–2 developers, 3–4 weeks
Trade-off: Costs ~2x more ($0.13/min vs $0.07/min) and loses warm transfer. Acceptable as a validation step before committing to a larger build.

Phase 2 — Scale Play (Months 2–5)

LK
Build on LiveKit Cloud
  • →Once VAPI validates that better providers solve the problem
  • →65% cost savings vs VAPI at scale ($0.046 vs $0.13/min)
  • →Zero vendor lock-in — own the full pipeline
  • →Warm transfer back via SIP REFER
  • →2+ engineers, 2–3 months
When to trigger: Once agent count exceeds 50+ or monthly minutes exceed 20K. The cost savings justify the engineering investment at that point.
🧪
Alternative: Bake-Off First
Before committing, run a side-by-side test call on VAPI and LiveKit with the same prompt. Compare voice quality + transcription accuracy. Takes 1–2 days, zero risk.
Next Steps

Decision & Action Items

Decisions Needed

1. Two-phase approach or direct to LiveKit?
VAPI first (fast, validates providers) → LiveKit later (cost optimization)
vs. invest upfront in LiveKit (slower, but skip the intermediate step)
2. Warm transfer priority?
If warm transfer is critical for clients, VAPI is disqualified — go directly to LiveKit, Pipecat, or Twilio CR
3. Engineering capacity?
VAPI: 1–2 devs, 3–4 weeks.
LiveKit: 2+ devs (Python), 2–3 months.
Do we have the bandwidth?
4. Budget tolerance?
VAPI doubles per-minute cost. LiveKit reduces it. What's the acceptable range while we're at 25 agents?

Immediate Action Items

1 Run a bake-off Set up a test call on VAPI with Deepgram Nova-3 + ElevenLabs. Compare transcription and voice quality against current Retell output. 1–2 days
2 Test LiveKit prototype Spin up a basic LiveKit Cloud agent with the same prompt. Assess voice quality and latency firsthand. 3–4 days
3 Decide & plan Based on bake-off results, choose platform and assign engineering team. 1 day
